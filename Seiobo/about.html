About Seiōbo

Since opening in 2011, Seiōbo has become one of the most unique dining experiences in Australia. Led by Executive Chef Paul Carmichael and General Manager Kylie Javier Ashton, the restaurant has become a place that hopes to transport its guests, combining the flavours of the Caribbean with the best Australian produce.
Part of the Momofuku group, Seiōbo was its first restaurant outside of New York City.

  • Seiōbo is currently ranked No. 2 restaurant in Australia by Gourmet Traveller Magazine 2020

  • Paul Carmichael was awarded of the Year by Gourmet Traveller Magazine 2020

  • Kylie Javier Ashton was awarded Citi Service Excellence Award by The Sydney Morning Herald Good Food Guide 2019

  • Momofuku Seiōbo has two hats from The Sydney Morning Herald Good Food Guide 2020

The Team

Paul Carmichael

Executive Chef

Paul is from Barbados where his first professional kitchen experiences were at Sandy Lane Hotel. After graduating from the Culinary Institute of America, Paul worked in kitchens such as Wd~50, Aquavit, Asiate, and the Tasting Room before serving as executive chef at Perla in Puerto Rico. He returned to New York in 2010 to join the Má Pêche team first as sous chef then as the executive chef. In 2015, Paul moved to Sydney to become the executive chef of Seiōbo. He was awarded Chef of the Year by Gourmet Traveller Restaurant Awards 2020 and Time Out Food Awards 2019.

Max Gürtler

Sommelier

Max was born in Berlin and moved to Australia in 2000. Starting his career in the kitchen as a chef, Max realised he had a passion for beverage and pursued a career in bartending working in some of Sydney’s top bars including the renowned Victoria Room. His interest in wine grew after working at Rockpool Group and has since focused his career as a Sommelier. He is currently studying for the CMS Master Sommelier Diploma. He started at Momofuku Seiōbo in 2017. In his spare time, he enjoys cooking and is a big Bayern Munich fan.

Kylie Javier Ashton

General Manager

Born and raised in Sydney, Kylie found her passion for restaurants working in some of Sydney’s best restaurants including Tetsuya’s and Bentley Restaurant & Bar. She has been part of many hospitality industry initiatives including the Taste of Young Sydney Collective, judging the Young Waiters competition for the national Appetite for Excellence program and is the Sydney coordinator for Grow Assembly. She was nominated for Gourmet Traveller Maître D’ of the Year 2018; was awarded Restaurant Manager of the Year in the inaugural Women in Foodservice Awards 2017 and received the National Service Excellence Award at the Good Food Guide Awards 2019. She joined Momofuku Seiōbo in 2012. When she’s not at Seiōbo, she can be found in a boxing gym.

FAQ's

  • What does Seiōbo mean?

    Seiōbo means ‘queen mother of the west,’ and her symbol is a peach.

  • What type of cuisine does Seiōbo serve?

    We try our best to serve delicious food celebrating the cuisine of the Caribbean through the voice of our team and the bounty of Australian produce.

  • Who is the chef at Seiōbo?

    Paul Carmichael is the Executive Chef of Seiōbo. David Chang is the Chef and Founder of Momofuku.

  • What’s on the menu?

    Seiōbo serves a tasting menu that will take you to places like Barbados, Jamaica, Puerto Rico and Cuba. We like to keep things fluid and fun, which means you won’t get a menu when you arrive. If you have dietary requirements, we are happy to accommodate, we just ask for a head’s up as our kitchen will need to design a menu especially for you.

  • Is there a difference between the early and later seating menu? Why are they different prices?

    We serve the same tasting menu for both seatings. Due to the time limit to accommodate the second seating, we have reflected this in the price of the menu at 5:45 pm.

  • Is bar food available?

    Unfortunately, bar food is not available. Seiōbo currently serves a tasting menu only. Seating times are 5:45pm and 8:30pm. Reservations are essential and can be made here.

  • Can you accommodate dietary restrictions and allergies?

    Yes. We will do our best to accommodate food allergies and dietary requirements. When making a reservation, please add your food allergies / restrictions to your reservation notes. It’s best if we know in advance, otherwise, we might not be able to accommodate a special menu.

  • Can I bring my kids? Is there a kid’s menu?

    Due to social distancing and capacity restrictions, we have a limited number of seats in the restaurant. Therefore, we only have the tasting menu available. If your kids would like to dine on the tasting menu, they are more than welcome to join us. Unfortunately, we do not have a separate kid’s menu and each person booked will be charged the full tasting menu price.

  • Do you have a pre-theatre menu? Can I do the tasting menu and be out in time for a 7:30pm show?

    Our tasting menu normally takes 2-2.5 hours to complete so we cannot guarantee you would be able to get out in time for the 7:30pm show. We only offer the 7-course menu and do not have any other pre-theatre options.
    We have had some guests complete the menu in a shorter amount of time. However, this is largely dependent on the individual guests’ pacing. If you would like to go ahead and make a booking in our 5:45pm seating, please make a note that you will need to be out by 7:25pm in order to be on time for the show (the Lyric Theatre is less than a 5 minute walk from the restaurant). We kindly request that you are on time for the booking, and please be mindful that we can cook and serve you only as quickly as you can eat.
    Due to the limitations on capacity and to reduce risk between seating times due to Covid, we are unable to offer for you to come back for dessert after the show. If you are unable to get through the meal in time for the show, we are happy to pack up any desserts for you to have during the intermission or to take home for later.

  • Is Seiōbo wheelchair accessible?

    Yes.

  • Do you take reservations? Do you allow walk-ins?

    Yes. We take reservations through our site. There are two seatings each night – 5:45pm and 8:30pm.
    Due to the limited size and capacity of the restaurant, we will only accept walk-ins in the case of a cancellation or if there is availability.

  • Why do you require my credit card details for the reservation?

    A cancellation fee of $200pp will be charged to your card in the case of cancellation with less than 24 hours’ notice and for no-shows.

  • What happens if I am sick? Will I still be charged a cancellation fee?

    If you are feeling unwell, please call us on (02) 9657 9169 and we would be happy to reschedule your booking for another date without any penalty fee.
    We are taking every precaution to keep our guests and our team safe in our restaurant. Anyone who is displaying symptoms of Covid-19 will be refused entry or asked to leave.

  • Can I have a private event at Seiōbo?

    Due to current government restrictions, we are unable to hold private events at Seiōbo. 10 people is the maximum number of guests we are able to accept as a booking.

  • Can I bring my own wine?

    Seiōbo is fully licensed and does not accept outside alcohol.

  • Can I bring my own cake?

    We do not accept outside cakes, but we are happy to provide a small cake for special celebrations. Please make a note of your occasion at the time of making your reservation.

  • Do you do take-out orders or delivery?

    No.

  • Do you sell gift cards?

    No. We do not sell Momofuku gift cards. The Star gift cards are accepted with prior Seiōbo reservation.
